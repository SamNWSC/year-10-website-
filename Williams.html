Williams

Image Credt: Wikimedia Commons

Williams Racing, officially known as Williams Grand Prix Engineering, is a storied British Formula One team and constructor founded by Sir Frank Williams and Sir Patrick Head. Since its inception in 1977, Williams has become one of the most successful teams in F1 history, with nine Constructors’ Championships and seven Drivers’ Championships1. Known for their competitive spirit and engineering excellence, Williams has achieved 114 race victories and is one of only five teams to have won over 100 races. The team’s legacy includes legendary drivers like Alan Jones, Keke Rosberg, Nigel Mansell, and Damon Hill, who have all won the Drivers’ title with Williams. With a commitment to racing and innovation, Williams continues to compete at the highest level, driven by a passion for motorsport that echoes through their decades-long presence in Formula One

Williams Drivrers

Alexander Albon

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Alexander Albon, born on March 23, 1996, in London, is a Thai-British racing driver competing under the Thai flag for Williams Racing in Formula One. Albon’s career began in karting, where he quickly established himself as a talented driver. He finished as runner-up in the 2016 GP3 Series and third in the 2018 FIA Formula 2 Championship before making his F1 debut with Toro Rosso in 2019. Mid-season, he was promoted to Red Bull Racing, partnering with Max Verstappen. Albon achieved his first podium at the 2020 Tuscan Grand Prix, marking the first World Championship F1 podium for a Thai driver. After a stint as a test and reserve driver for Red Bull, he returned to the grid with Williams in 2022, continuing with the team in 2023. Known for his fast qualifying and mature racing style, Albon remains a prominent figure in F1, showcasing resilience and a championship mentality

Logan Saregant

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Logan Sargeant, an American racing prodigy born on December 31, 2000, has made a significant impact in Formula One as a driver for Williams Racing. Starting his motorsport career in karting in 2008, Sargeant quickly moved up the ranks, winning the CIK-FIA World KFJ Championship in 2015 and making his mark in single-seater racing with podium finishes in the F4 UAE Championship and British F4. His transition to higher formulas saw him finishing fourth in the 2022 FIA Formula 2 Championship with Carlin Motorsport. Sargeant’s entry into Formula One in 2023 marked the return of an American driver to the grid, and with races in Miami, Austin, and Las Vegas, he’s sure to have plenty of home support as he continues to chase success at the pinnacle of motorsport
