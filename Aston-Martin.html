Aston Martin

Image Credit: paulwalker, Goodfon

Aston Martin’s journey in Formula One is a blend of heritage and resurgence. The British car manufacturer first entered the sport in 1959 with the DBR4 chassis and its own engine, but after a lack of success, they withdrew after the 1960 season. Decades later, the iconic brand made a comeback to F1 in 2021, rebranded from the Racing Point F1 Team and utilizing customer Mercedes power units. Looking ahead, Aston Martin has announced a works partnership with Honda for power units starting in 2026. With Lawrence Stroll at the helm and drivers like Fernando Alonso and Lance Stroll, the team is headquartered in Silverstone and is on a mission to carve out a new chapter of success in the pinnacle of motorsport


Aston Martins Drivers

Fernando Alonso

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Fernando Alonso, a name synonymous with passion and precision in Formula One, is a Spanish racing driver currently competing for Aston Martin. Born on July 29, 1981, in Oviedo, Asturias, Spain, Alonso’s career is decorated with two World Drivers’ Championship titles in 2005 and 2006 with Renault. Known for his aggressive driving style and strategic acumen, Alonso has driven for top teams including McLaren, Ferrari, and Minardi. His return to F1 with Aston Martin has been met with much anticipation, and rumors suggest he may partner with Carlos Sainz in 2025. Despite not having won a race since 2013, Alonso’s experience and skill continue to make him a formidable presence on the grid

Lance Stroll

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Lance Stroll, born on October 29, 1998, in Montreal, Quebec, Canada, is a professional racing driver who competes under the Canadian flag in Formula One, currently driving for Aston Martin. Stroll’s career began in karting at a young age, and he quickly made a name for himself by winning the Italian F4 Championship in 2014, the Toyota Racing Series in 2015, and the FIA European Formula 3 Championship in 2016. He joined the Ferrari Driver Academy and made his Formula One debut with Williams in 2017, achieving his first podium finish at the Azerbaijan Grand Prix the same year. Known for his resilience and racing prowess, Stroll took his first pole position at the 2020 Turkish Grand Prix. As the son of billionaire businessman Lawrence Stroll, Lance has faced scrutiny but continues to prove his skill on the track
