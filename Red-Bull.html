Red Bull Racing

Image Credit: pcsldot, Flickr

Red Bull Racing, officially known as Oracle Red Bull Racing, is a Formula One racing team that has established itself as a dominant force in the sport. Founded in 2004 after Red Bull’s acquisition of Jaguar Racing, the team is based in Milton Keynes, England. Red Bull Racing has achieved remarkable success, including six World Constructors’ Championships and numerous race victories. With star drivers like Max Verstappen and Sergio Perez, the team has continued to excel, with Verstappen securing a record-breaking 15 wins in the 2022 season and leading the team to a championship double. Celebrating over two decades in F1, Red Bull Racing remains committed to excellence and innovation, consistently challenging for titles and pushing the boundaries of the sport


Red Bull's Drivers

Max Verstappen

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Max Verstappen, born on September 30, 1997, is a Belgian-Dutch racing driver who competes under the Dutch flag for Red Bull Racing in Formula One. As the son of former F1 driver Jos Verstappen, Max has racing in his blood. He made history as the youngest driver to compete in F1 at the age of 17 and quickly rose to prominence with his aggressive driving style and impressive skill. Verstappen became the youngest-ever Grand Prix winner with his victory at the 2016 Spanish Grand Prix. He has since claimed three World Championships in 2021, 2022, and 2023, setting records for the most wins in a season and the most consecutive wins. As of the 2024 Australian Grand Prix, Verstappen has achieved 56 victories, 35 pole positions, and 31 fastest laps. He is set to remain with Red Bull Racing until at least the end of the 2028 season

Sergio Perez

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Sergio Pérez, also known as “Checo,” born on January 26, 1990, in Guadalajara, Mexico, is a Formula One racing driver currently competing for Red Bull Racing. Pérez has been a part of the F1 grid since 2011, having driven for teams like Sauber, McLaren, Force India, and Racing Point before joining Red Bull. Known for his tenacity on the track and his ability to manage tires effectively, Pérez has achieved six Grand Prix victories and numerous podium finishes throughout his career. His first win at the 2020 Sakhir Grand Prix was a breakthrough moment, and he has since become a key player in Red Bull’s championship campaigns. Off the track, Pérez is a family man, married with four children, and is known for his charitable work in his home country
