Sauber

Image Credit: Vector Portal

Sauber Motorsport, a Swiss motorsport engineering company, has been a fixture in Formula One since its debut in the 1993 South African Grand Prix. Founded by Peter Sauber in 1970, the team has seen various partnerships and name changes over the years, including a notable stint as BMW Sauber from 2006 to 2010, where they achieved their only Grand Prix victory at the 2008 Canadian Grand Prix. In recent developments, Audi has acquired 100% of Sauber, with plans to rebrand the team for its official entry into Formula One in 2026. Despite challenges, such as pitstop issues in the 2024 season, Sauber’s commitment to F1 remains steadfast, with the team continuing to compete under the name Stake F1 Team Kick Sauber. Sauber’s legacy in F1 is marked by resilience and adaptability, reflecting a deep-rooted passion for racing


Saubers Drivers

P17 Valtteri Bottas

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Valtteri Bottas, born on August 28, 1989, in Nastola, Finland, is a seasoned Formula One driver currently competing for Stake F1 Team Kick Sauber. Bottas began his F1 career with Williams in 2013, where he demonstrated his potential with nine podium finishes. His performance earned him a seat at Mercedes, replacing Nico Rosberg in 2017. During his tenure at Mercedes, Bottas secured 10 race wins and contributed to five Constructors’ Championship victories, finishing as the Drivers’ Championship runner-up in 2019 and 2020. Known for his calm demeanor and technical driving style, Bottas has been a consistent points scorer throughout his career. After a stint with Mercedes, he joined Alfa Romeo, now rebranded as Sauber, where he continues to showcase his racing prowess

P16 Zhou Guanyu

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Zhou Guanyu, born on May 30, 1999, in Shanghai, China, is a trailblazer in Formula One as the first Chinese driver to compete in the World Championship. He began karting at eight and moved to the UK for a more competitive environment, winning the Super 1 National Rotax Max Junior Championship and the Rotax Max Euro Challenge. Zhou’s single-seater career started with success in the Italian F4 Championship, leading to a stint in the FIA Formula 3 European Championship and then the FIA Formula 2 Championship, where he finished third in 2021. A former member of the Ferrari Driver Academy and the Alpine Academy, Zhou made his F1 debut with Alfa Romeo in 2022 and continues to race for Stake F1 Team Kick Sauber. His journey from karting prodigy to F1 competitor marks a significant milestone for motorsport in China
