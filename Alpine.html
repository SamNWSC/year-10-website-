Alpine

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Alpine F1 Team, currently known as BWT Alpine F1 Team for sponsorship reasons, represents the latest chapter in the storied history of Renault’s involvement in Formula One. The team, which competes with a French license, is the rebranded identity of the Renault F1 Team, a transformation that took place at the start of the 2021 Formula One World Championship. Alpine, Renault’s sports car brand, has a rich motorsport heritage, and the rebranding was a strategic move to promote this marque on the global F1 stage. The team’s operations are split between Enstone, England, for the chassis and managerial side, and Viry-Châtillon, France, for the Renault-branded engine side. Despite not having won a Constructors’ Championship under the Alpine name, the team has achieved race victories and podium finishes, showcasing their competitive spirit and technical capabilities. With drivers like Esteban Ocon and Pierre Gasly, Alpine aims to build on its legacy and become title contenders in the pinnacle of motorsport


Alpines Drivers

Pierre Gasly

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Pierre Gasly, born on February 7, 1996, in Rouen, France, is a French racing driver currently competing in Formula One for Alpine. His career highlights include winning the 2016 GP2 Series and finishing as runner-up in the 2014 Formula Renault 3.5 Series and the 2017 Super Formula Championship. Gasly made his F1 debut with Toro Rosso at the 2017 Malaysian Grand Prix and achieved his first podium at the 2019 Brazilian Grand Prix. His maiden victory came at the 2020 Italian Grand Prix while driving for AlphaTauri (currently known as VCARB), marking both his and the team’s first win in Formula One. In 2023, Gasly earned his fourth career podium with a third-place finish at the Dutch Grand Prix. Known for his resilience and skill, Gasly continues to be a prominent figure in the sport

Estaban Ocon

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Esteban Ocon, born on September 17, 1996, in Évreux, France, is a French racing driver currently competing in Formula One for Alpine. Ocon’s journey to F1 is marked by sacrifice and determination; his parents sold their house to support his early karting career. He won the 2014 FIA Formula 3 European Championship and the 2015 GP3 Series, which led to a role in the Mercedes driver development programme. Ocon made his F1 debut with Manor Racing in 2016 and has since raced for Force India/Racing Point and Renault, which became Alpine in 2021. His maiden F1 victory came at the 2021 Hungarian Grand Prix. Despite not winning a race in 2022, he secured his best position in the drivers’ standings in eighth, contributing to Alpine’s fourth place in the Constructors’ Championship1. Ocon’s resilience and talent continue to shine in the competitive world of Formula One
