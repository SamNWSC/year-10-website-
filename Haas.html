Haas

Image Credit: Leo Reynolds, Flickr

Haas F1 Team, an American-licensed racing team, made its debut in the 2016 Formula One season. Established by NASCAR Cup Series team co-owner Gene Haas, the team is based in Kannapolis, North Carolina, with a European base in Banbury, England. Despite a challenging start, Haas F1 has shown resilience and determination in the highly competitive world of Formula One. With drivers Kevin Magnussen and Nico Hülkenberg at the helm for the 2024 season, the team continues to strive for its first podium finish, leveraging a strong partnership with Ferrari for engines and technical support. As they progress, Haas F1 Team remains focused on climbing the ranks and making a mark in the pinnacle of motorsport


Haas's Drivers

Kevin Magnussen

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Kevin Magnussen, born on October 5, 1992, in Roskilde, Denmark, is a Formula One driver known for his tenacity and skill on the racetrack. Currently competing for Haas F1 Team, Magnussen has been a part of the Formula One world since his debut with McLaren in 2014, where he made an immediate impact by securing a second-place finish in his first race. Despite facing ups and downs throughout his career, including a stint with Renault and a brief departure from F1 to compete in other racing series, Magnussen’s return to Haas in 2022 was marked by securing the team’s first-ever pole position at the Brazilian Grand Prix. His aggressive driving style and determination to succeed have made him a standout figure in the sport, as he continues to push for excellence with each race

Nico Hulkenberg

\

Image Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Nico Hülkenberg, a seasoned German racing driver, has been a persistent figure in Formula One, known for his solid performances and technical prowess. Born on August 19, 1987, in Emmerich am Rhein, Germany, Hülkenberg’s career highlights include winning the 2009 GP2 Series and the prestigious 24 Hours of Le Mans in 2015 with Porsche on his first attempt. Despite his success, he holds the unfortunate record for the most Formula One career starts without a podium finish. Hülkenberg’s journey through various teams, including Williams, Force India, Sauber, and Renault, showcases his adaptability and skill. In 2023, he returned to a full-time F1 seat with Haas F1 Team, bringing a wealth of experience and a reputation for being a highly valued team player. His career demonstrates not just longevity but also the respect he commands in the paddock for his dedication and racing intellect.
